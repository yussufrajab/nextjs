## 1. Welcome to CSMS

### 1.1 What is CSMS?

The **Civil Service Management System (CSMS)** is Zanzibar's comprehensive platform for managing the entire lifecycle of civil service employees. The system streamlines HR processes from hiring through separation, including:

- Employee confirmations after probation
- Promotions (experience-based and education-based)
- Leave Without Pay (LWOP) requests
- Cadre changes
- Retirements (voluntary, compulsory, illness-based)
- Resignations
- Service extensions
- Terminations and dismissals
- Employee complaints and grievances

### 1.2 Who Uses CSMS?

CSMS serves **9 different user roles**:

| Role         | Who You Are               | What You Do                                           |
| ------------ | ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| **HRO**      | HR Officer                | Submit HR requests for your institution               |
| **HHRMD**    | Head of HR & Disciplinary | Approve all HR and disciplinary matters               |
| **HRMO**     | HR Management Officer     | Approve HR requests (confirmations, promotions, etc.) |
| **DO**       | Disciplinary Officer      | Handle complaints and terminations                    |
| **PO**       | Planning Officer          | View reports and analytics                            |
| **CSCS**     | CSC Secretary             | Executive oversight of all activities                 |
| **HRRP**     | HR Responsible Personnel  | Institutional HR supervisor                           |
| **ADMIN**    | Administrator             | Manage users, institutions, and system                |
| **EMPLOYEE** | Civil Service Employee    | View profile and submit complaints                    |

### 1.3 System Access

**Main Portal:** https://csms.zanajira.go.tz
**Employee Portal:** https://csms.zanajira.go.tz/employee-login

## 3. Essential Tasks by Role

### 3.1 HR Officer (HRO) - Essential Tasks

#### Task 1: Submit a Confirmation Request

**When:** Employee has completed probation period (typically 1 year)

**Steps:**

1. Navigate to **Confirmations** module
2. Click **New Confirmation Request**
3. **Select Employee**:
   - Choose employee with status "On Probation"
   - Only employees from your institution appear
4. **Upload Documents** (PDF only, max 2MB):
   - Performance evaluation
   - Supervisor recommendation
   - Any supporting documents
5. **Review** all information
6. Click **Submit**
7. **Confirmation**: You'll see "Request submitted successfully"
8. **Notification**: HHRMD and DO are automatically notified

**What Happens Next:**

- Request status: "Pending"
- HHRMD or HRMO will review and approve/reject
- You'll receive a notification when decision is made
- If approved: Employee status changes to "Confirmed"

#### Task 2: Submit a Promotion Request

**Types of Promotions:**

1. **Experience-based**: Requires 3 years of performance appraisals
2. **Education-based**: Requires educational certificates

**Steps:**

1. Navigate to **Promotions** module
2. Click **New Promotion Request**
3. **Select Employee** (must be "Confirmed" status)
4. **Choose Promotion Type**:
   - Experience-based OR
   - Education-based
5. **Enter Proposed Cadre**: New position/rank
6. **Upload Required Documents**:

   **For Experience-based:**
   - Performance appraisal Year 1 (PDF)
   - Performance appraisal Year 2 (PDF)
   - Performance appraisal Year 3 (PDF)
   - CSC promotion form (PDF)
   - Letter of request (PDF)

   **For Education-based:**
   - Educational certificate (PDF)
   - TCU form (if studied outside country)
   - Letter of request (PDF)

7. **Check "Studied Outside Country"** if applicable
8. Click **Submit**

**What Happens Next:**

- HHRMD or HRMO reviews
- If approved: Employee cadre updated to proposed cadre
- You receive notification of decision

#### Task 3: Submit LWOP (Leave Without Pay) Request

**When:** Employee needs unpaid leave

**Steps:**

1. Navigate to **LWOP** module
2. Click **New LWOP Request**
3. **Select Employee** (cannot be already on LWOP)
4. **Enter Start Date**: When LWOP begins
5. **Enter End Date**: When LWOP ends
6. **Enter Duration**: e.g., "6 months", "1 year"
7. **Enter Reason**: Detailed explanation
8. **Upload Documents**: Supporting documents
9. Click **Submit**

**Important:**

- End date must be after start date
- Employee cannot be already "On LWOP"

**What Happens Next:**

- HHRMD or HRMO reviews
- If approved: Employee status changes to "On LWOP"

#### Task 4: Track Request Status

**Steps:**

1. Navigate to **Track Status** module
2. **View All Requests**: See all your submitted requests
3. **Filter**:
   - By request type (Confirmation, Promotion, etc.)
   - By status (Pending, Approved, Rejected)
   - By date range
4. **Click on a Request**: View detailed information
5. **Check Timeline**: See submission, review, decision dates
6. **View Comments**: See approver comments (if rejected)

### 3.2 HHRMD (Head of HR & Disciplinary) - Essential Tasks

#### Task 1: Review and Approve Confirmation Request

**Steps:**

1. Go to **Dashboard** ‚Üí **Pending Approvals**
2. Click on **Confirmations**
3. **Select a Request** to review
4. **Review Information**:
   - Employee name and details
   - Current status (should be "On Probation")
   - Submission date
   - Submitted by (HRO name)
5. **Download and Review Documents**:
   - Click on each document
   - Review in PDF viewer or download
6. **Make Decision**:

   **To Approve:**
   - Click **Approve**
   - Add comments (optional)
   - Click **Confirm**
   - Result: Status ‚Üí "Approved by Commission"
   - Employee status ‚Üí "Confirmed"
   - Confirmation date recorded

   **To Reject:**
   - Click **Reject**
   - **Enter Rejection Reason** (required)
   - Click **Confirm**
   - Result: Status ‚Üí "Rejected"
   - Employee status unchanged
   - HRO notified with reason

**What Happens Next:**

- HRO receives notification
- Request appears in history
- If approved: Employee can now be promoted, etc.

#### Task 2: Review Promotion Request

**Steps:**

1. Navigate to **Promotions** module
2. Click on pending request
3. **Review Details**:
   - Employee current cadre
   - Proposed cadre
   - Promotion type (Experience or Education)
   - "Studied Outside Country" flag
4. **Review Documents**:
   - Check all required documents uploaded
   - Verify authenticity
   - For experience: Check 3 years of appraisals
   - For education: Verify certificates
5. **Make Decision**:

   **To Approve:**
   - Click **Approve**
   - Employee cadre updates to proposed cadre

   **To Reject:**
   - Click **Reject**
   - Enter detailed reason
   - Employee cadre remains unchanged

#### Task 3: Handle Complaint

**Steps:**

1. Navigate to **Complaints** module
2. **View Complaint Details**:
   - Case ID (unique identifier)
   - Complaint type
   - Subject (min 5 characters)
   - Details (min 20 characters)
   - Complainant phone number
   - Next of kin phone number
   - Attachments (if any)
3. **Review Attachments**: Download and review
4. **Assign Officer** (optional):
   - Select officer role to handle
   - Officer is notified
5. **Take Action**:

   **Option 1: Resolve Complaint**
   - Add resolution notes
   - Mark as "Resolved"
   - Employee notified in Swahili

   **Option 2: Request More Information**
   - Select "Request More Info"
   - Enter what information is needed
   - Status: "More Info Requested"
   - Employee can respond

**Complaint Status:**

- Pending ‚Üí Under Review ‚Üí Resolved
- OR
- Pending ‚Üí More Info Requested ‚Üí Under Review ‚Üí Resolved

## 4. Common Workflows

### 4.1 Complete Employee Confirmation Workflow

**Scenario:** Employee has completed 1 year probation period

**Timeline:** 2-5 business days

**Participants:**

- **HRO** (HR Officer): Submits request
- **HHRMD or HRMO**: Approves/rejects
- **Employee**: Benefits from confirmation

**Workflow:**

```
[HRO: Submit] ‚Üí [HHRMD/HRMO: Review] ‚Üí [Decision] ‚Üí [Employee: Status Updated]
```

**Detailed Steps:**

**Day 1 - HRO Submits Request:**

1. HRO logs in
2. Goes to Confirmations module
3. Clicks "New Confirmation Request"
4. Selects employee (must be "On Probation")
5. Uploads required documents:
   - Performance evaluation (PDF)
   - Supervisor recommendation (PDF)
6. Submits request
7. **Result**:
   - Request status: "Pending"
   - Notification sent to HHRMD and DO

**Day 2-3 - HHRMD/HRMO Reviews:**

1. HHRMD/HRMO receives notification
2. Logs in and views pending approvals
3. Clicks on confirmation request
4. Reviews:
   - Employee information
   - Documents
   - Submission date
5. Downloads and verifies documents

**Day 4 - Decision Made:**

**If Approved:**

- HHRMD/HRMO clicks "Approve"
- Adds comments (optional)
- Confirms approval
- **System Actions**:
  - Request status ‚Üí "Approved by Commission"
  - Employee status ‚Üí "Confirmed"
  - Confirmation date recorded
  - HRO notified
  - Employee record updated

**If Rejected:**

- HHRMD/HRMO clicks "Reject"
- Enters rejection reason (required)
- Confirms rejection
- **System Actions**:
  - Request status ‚Üí "Rejected"
  - Employee status remains "On Probation"
  - HRO notified with reason
  - Can resubmit after addressing issues

**Day 5 - Follow-up:**

- HRO checks notification
- Reviews decision
- If rejected: Addresses issues and resubmits
- If approved: Employee can now be promoted

### 4.2 Promotion Request Workflow (Experience-Based)

**Scenario:** Employee with 3 years good performance eligible for promotion

**Timeline:** 5-10 business days

**Participants:**

- **HRO**: Submits request
- **HHRMD or HRMO**: Approves/rejects
- **Employee**: Receives promotion

**Workflow:**

```
[HRO: Prepare Documents] ‚Üí [HRO: Submit] ‚Üí [HHRMD/HRMO: Review] ‚Üí [Decision] ‚Üí [Cadre Updated]
```

**Detailed Steps:**

**Week 1 - Day 1: HRO Prepares**

1. Collect required documents:
   - Performance appraisal Year 1 (PDF)
   - Performance appraisal Year 2 (PDF)
   - Performance appraisal Year 3 (PDF)
   - CSC promotion form (PDF)
   - Letter of request (PDF)
2. Ensure all documents are PDF format, under 2MB each
3. Verify employee is "Confirmed" status (not On Probation, LWOP, or separated)

**Week 1 - Day 2: HRO Submits**

1. Navigate to Promotions module
2. Click "New Promotion Request"
3. Select employee
4. Choose "Experience-based" promotion type
5. Enter proposed cadre (new position)
6. Upload all 5 documents
7. Submit request
8. **Result**: Notification sent to HHRMD and DO

**Week 1 - Day 3-5: HHRMD/HRMO Reviews**

1. Receives notification
2. Views promotion request
3. Reviews:
   - Current cadre
   - Proposed cadre
   - All 3 years of appraisals
   - CSC form and request letter
4. Verifies:
   - Employee eligibility
   - Performance meets standards
   - Proposed cadre appropriate
5. May request additional information

**Week 2 - Day 8: Decision**

**If Approved:**

- Click "Approve"
- Add comments
- **System Actions**:

- Click "Reject"
- Enter detailed reason
- **System Actions**:
  - Request status ‚Üí "Rejected"

  - HRO notified with reason

**Week 2 - Day 10: Verification**

- HRO verifies employee record
- Confirms cadre updated
- Documents filed

### 4.3 LWOP Request and Return Workflow

**Scenario:** Employee needs 6 months unpaid leave, then returns

**Timeline:** Request: 3-5 days, Duration: 6 months, Return: 1 day

**Workflow:**

```
[Request LWOP] ‚Üí [Approval] ‚Üí [6 months] ‚Üí [Return to Work] ‚Üí [Status Update]
```

**Phase 1: LWOP Request (Days 1-5)**

**Day 1 - HRO Submits:**

1. Navigate to LWOP module
2. Create new LWOP request
3. Select employee (must not be already on LWOP)
4. Enter details:
   - Start date: e.g., March 1, 2026
   - End date: e.g., August 31, 2026
   - Duration: "6 months"
   - Reason: Detailed explanation
5. Upload supporting documents
6. Submit

**Day 3 - HHRMD/HRMO Reviews:**

1. Reviews request
2. Verifies dates (end date after start date)
3. Reviews reason
4. Makes decision

**Day 5 - Approval:**

- If approved:
  - Employee status ‚Üí "On LWOP"
  - Start date recorded
  - HRO notified

**Phase 2: LWOP Period (6 months)**

- Employee on leave without pay
- Status in system: "On LWOP"
- Cannot submit other requests while on LWOP
- Cannot be promoted while on LWOP

**Phase 3: Return to Work**

**End of 6 Months:**

1. HRO submits "Return from LWOP" request or administrative action
2. HHRMD/HRMO approves return
3. **System Actions**:
   - Employee status ‚Üí "Confirmed" (or previous status)
   - End date recorded
   - Employee can now be promoted, etc.

### 4.4 Employee Complaint Resolution Workflow

**Scenario:** Employee has grievance, submits complaint, receives resolution

**Timeline:** 10-30 business days (depending on complexity)

**Participants:**

- **Employee**: Submits complaint
- **HHRMD or DO**: Reviews and resolves
- **Assigned Officer**: May be assigned to handle

**Workflow:**

```
[Employee: Submit] ‚Üí [HHRMD/DO: Review] ‚Üí [Assign Officer (optional)] ‚Üí [Investigate] ‚Üí [Resolve]
```

**Detailed Steps:**

**Day 1 - Employee Submits Complaint:**

1. Employee logs in via employee portal
2. Navigates to Complaints
3. Clicks "Submit Complaint"
4. Fills in form:
   - Complaint type: Select from dropdown
   - Subject: "Unfair Treatment in Promotion Process" (example)
   - Details: Detailed explanation (min 20 characters)
   - Phone number: Contact number
   - Next of kin phone: Emergency contact
   - Attachments: Upload evidence (optional)
5. Submits
6. **Result**:
   - Case ID generated (e.g., CMP-2025-00123)
   - Status: "Pending"
   - Notification sent to HHRMD and DO (in Swahili)

**Day 2 - HHRMD/DO Acknowledges:**

1. Receives notification
2. Views complaint
3. Reviews:
   - Case ID
   - Complaint type
   - Subject and details
   - Attachments
4. Assesses severity and type

**Day 3 - Assignment (if needed):**

1. HHRMD/DO decides to assign
2. Selects officer role to handle
3. Adds internal notes
4. Officer receives notification

**Day 5-15 - Investigation:**

1. Assigned officer investigates
2. May request more information:
   - Clicks "Request More Info"
   - Enters what info is needed
   - Employee notified (in Swahili)
   - Status ‚Üí "More Info Requested"
3. Employee responds with additional information
4. Officer continues investigation
5. Adds internal notes (not visible to employee)

**Day 20 - Resolution:**

1. Officer/HHRMD/DO makes determination
2. Adds resolution notes
3. Marks as "Resolved"
4. **System Actions**:
   - Status ‚Üí "Resolved"
   - Employee notified (in Swahili)
   - Resolution visible to employee
   - Case closed

**Day 21 - Follow-up:**

- Employee views resolution
- Can see officer comments
- Case history maintained

### 7.2 Common Questions

#### Q: I forgot my password. What do I do?

**A:** Contact your system administrator. They can reset your password.

#### Q: I can't see employees from other institutions. Is this a bug?

**A:** No. If you're an HRO or HRRP, you can only see employees from your own institution. This is by design for data security. Only CSC roles (HHRMD, HRMO, DO, PO, CSCS) and ADMIN can see all institutions.

#### Q: Why can't I upload my Word document?

**A:** The system only accepts PDF files. Convert your Word document to PDF first, then upload. Make sure it's under 2MB.

#### Q: My request was rejected. Can I resubmit?

**A:** Yes! Read the rejection reason carefully, address the issues mentioned, and submit a new request with corrected information.

#### Q: How long does approval take?

**A:** Typical approval time is 3-7 business days, depending on request complexity and approver availability. Check your notifications for updates.

#### Q: Can I edit a request after submission?

**A:** No. Once submitted, requests cannot be edited. If you need to make changes, contact the approver or submit a new request.

#### Q: I submitted the wrong document. What should I do?

**A:** Contact the approver immediately via your institution's communication channel. They may reject the request, and you can resubmit with correct documents.

#### Q: Why is the status in Swahili?

**A:** CSMS is bilingual. Reports and some statuses show in Swahili to support all users. Common statuses:

- **Imekamilika** = Approved/Completed
- **Inasubiri** = Pending
- **Imekataliwa** = Rejected

#### Q: Can I access CSMS on my phone?

**A:** Yes! CSMS is mobile-friendly. Use your phone's web browser to access the same URL.

#### Q: What browser should I use?

**A:** Chrome is recommended, but Firefox, Edge, and Safari also work well. Make sure JavaScript and cookies are enabled.
